Fun things to do in Barranquilla as a group

Paulette Romero
August 17, 2023 — Updated April 23, 2026 · 9 min read

Barranquilla, a vibrant city located on the northern coast of Colombia, offers a plethora of exciting activities that are perfect for groups looking to have a blast together. From heart-pounding adventures to relaxed bonding sessions, there’s something for every group to enjoy in this lively city. Whether you’re seeking thrills or relaxation, Barranquilla has a variety of activities.

Xtreme Park Barranquilla

For those seeking an adrenaline rush, Xtreme Park Barranquilla is an absolute must-visit. This adventure park is packed with thrilling attractions that cater to all levels of daredevils. Test your courage on an ATV or dirtbike, or challenge your friends to a thrilling go-kart race on the high-speed tracks. Xtreme Park promises an unforgettable adventure for your group.

Bowling and Batting

Get ready for some friendly competition at Barranquilla’s bowling alleys and batting zones. Strike down pins in exhilarating bowling matches at Bolos Moñona on 51B. If swinging a bat is more your style, you can unleash your inner slugger at Batting Zone where you can practice your swing. These activities offer a laid-back yet engaging environment for groups to enjoy some laughs, improve their skills, and bond over shared victories and strikes.

Yoga on the Beach

For those seeking a more tranquil experience, Barranquilla’s stunning beaches – including Puerto Mocho – provide the perfect setting for group yoga sessions. Imagine starting your day with the sun rising over the Caribbean Sea as you flow through calming yoga poses on the sandy shores. Whether your group is new to yoga or consists of experienced practitioners, the beach’s serene atmosphere will help you find inner peace while fostering a deeper connection with your fellow group members. Sunday morning yoga with yogi Susana Alzate at Casa Surff in Puerto Colombia is a relaxing group activity.

Bowling and Billiards at Spinning Center Club – Buenavista 2

You may know Spinning Center as only a gym, but did you know they also have an entertainment area that includes bowling, billiards and a restaurant-bar? Head to the new Spinning Center CLUB on the fourth floor of Buenavista 2. You don’t have to be a member of the gym. All members of the public can enjoy the bowling facilities or the dining and bar options. One hour of bowling will set you back $120,000 COP for up to 6 people.

Sip & Paint

Artistic types will enjoy the Sip & Paint events held by La Pallete Art Lab. Gather a few friends together and spend a relaxing afternoon painting, sipping wine, and eating healthy snacks. Some past painting activities include a groovy neon night with glow-in-the-dark paint, painting flower vases, and even artistic embroidery.

Escape Room Barranquilla

If your group enjoys brain-teasing challenges and problem-solving, Barranquilla’s Escape Room will keep you engaged and entertained. Gather your friends and immerse yourselves in intricate scenarios that require teamwork, creativity, and quick thinking to solve puzzles and escape within a set time limit. With Halloween fast approaching you’ll want to add this activity to your list of things to do.

Catch a Baseball Game

Baseball enthusiasts in your group (check out more popular sports in Barranquilla) will find themselves in paradise at the Edgar Renteria Baseball Stadium. Catching a live game here is an experience that goes beyond the sport itself. The cheers of the crowd, the crack of the bat, and the electric atmosphere as you support the home team create memories that will last a lifetime. Baseball season starts up again in early November. Be sure to catch a game featuring the local team, the Caimanes de Barranquilla.

Paintball

For a high-energy group activity that combines strategy and excitement, paintball is the perfect choice. One of the most popular paintball locations is Paintball House in Alameda del Rio. Whether you’re a paintball pro or a newbie, the heart-pounding action and teamwork required will undoubtedly create unforgettable memories for your group.

Bourbon St Rock Bar

One of the highlights of Bourbon St Rock Bar is its dedication to live music. From local talents to touring bands, the stage at Bourbon St has witnessed electrifying performances that span various subgenres of rock. What truly sets Bourbon St Rock Bar apart is its genuine commitment to supporting the local music scene. The establishment often hosts events that showcase emerging local talents. The band performs live every weekend so grab some friends and head to Bourbon for a fun night of live music.

Karaoke!

Want to belt out some tunes? Fabio Restobar has two private cavas you and your friends can book to spend the night singing karaoke. Food and drinks are also available. The diverse song selection caters to various musical tastes, spanning genres from timeless classics to current chart-toppers. Whether you’re a rock ‘n’ roll enthusiast or a pop diva at heart, you’re sure to find a track that resonates with your musical sensibilities.

Caribe Aventura Water Park

If you’re seeking an adrenaline-pumping adventure, look no further than the Caribe Aventura Water Park. This sprawling aquatic paradise is a perfect destination for families and thrill-seekers alike. With an array of water slides, wave pools, and lazy rivers, the park promises endless hours of excitement and relaxation. Whizz down twisting slides or soak up the sun on a tranquil poolside, all within 45 minutes from Barranquilla.

Go on a walking tour

El City Lover offers a fun ghost walking tour every weekend, among others. Visit the large mansions built in El Prado at the turn of the 20th century and soak up the ghost stories. An intermediate Spanish level is recommended. You can also book a guided walking tour of El Prado, or go on a walking tour of Barrio Abajo, a very traditional Barranquilla neighborhood, with Discovery Experiences by Ana Cotes. If your Spanish isn’t the best, no worries, reach out to Ana and she can give you a tour in English! Ana also offers private custom-made tours.

La Ruta del Girasol: The Sunflower Fields

The tourist and gastronomic stop called ‘Gira entre soles’ has returned to Baranoa, offering a tourist space open to both locals and visitors. In addition to sunflowers, you’ll get to enjoy local gastronomy and crafts. Furthermore, there will be opportunities for children to interact with animals and take pony rides. La Ruta del Girasol sunflower fields can be visited from 8:00 a.m. to 6:00 p.m. The cost for visitors is 10,000 pesos per person, and children under 10 years of age have free admission.

Go to a Food, Drink, or Music Festival

Carnaval de Verano: Río y Mar

In Barranquilla, the spirit of Carnival, the country’s most emblematic cultural celebration, never rests. Each year a mid-year edition extends the festivities for ‘curramberos’ and visitors alike. Carnaval de Verano: Río y Mar typically runs over a weekend in late June. Friday programming at the Gran Malecón features a knowledge exchange on the dances that make Carnival a heritage of humanity, and Saturday closes with the Summer Music Festival at Plaza de La Paz, spotlighting national orchestras. Dates for each year’s edition are announced a few weeks in advance.

Sabor Barranquilla Festival: A Gastronomic Fiesta

For food enthusiasts, the Sabor Barranquilla Festival is a feast for the senses. This culinary extravaganza showcases the diverse flavors of the Caribbean region, highlighting local ingredients and traditional dishes. From succulent seafood platters to mouthwatering tropical fruit desserts, Sabor Barranquilla brings together talented chefs, food vendors, and gastronomes to indulge in a celebration of the city’s gastronomic heritage. Sabor Barranquilla typically runs over four days in late August; exact dates are announced closer to the event.

Timeless Music Festival: Get Funky

Timeless is the leading festival for funky, indie, and alternative music. Past headliners have included Aterciopelados, Mitú, MNKYBSNSS and The Satanic Majesties Request. The event takes place on the Gran Malecón and promotes emerging artists. If you’re looking for something alternative, outside of typical mainstream music, then head over to Timeless Festival! Timeless is a one-day festival usually held in late summer; upcoming dates are announced by the organizers.

Expodrinks Festival: Toasting to Diversity

Kicking off the festival lineup is the Expodrinks Festival. It’s a celebration of beverages that embody the country’s diverse and colorful spirit. From traditional Colombian Aguardiente to imported wines and artisanal beers, Expodrinks provides a platform for local and regional producers to showcase their creations. This festival is not only about sipping on delightful concoctions but also offers educational sessions on brewing techniques. Expodrinks runs over four days at Puerta de Oro, typically in early October; upcoming dates are announced by the organizers.

Barranquijazz Festival: A Melodious Journey

Barranquijazz is a world-renowned music festival that resonates with the soulful rhythms of jazz. This event gathers both national and international jazz artists who take the stage to create an electrifying atmosphere. Barranquijazz isn’t just about the music – it’s a celebration of the city’s love for artistic expression. Workshops, masterclasses, and jam sessions offer budding musicians and enthusiasts the chance to learn from seasoned professionals. Barranquijazz runs over five days each September; upcoming dates are announced by the organizers.

In conclusion…

Barranquilla is a city that caters to the diverse interests of groups seeking memorable experiences. From heart-pounding adventures at Xtreme Park and challenging escape room quests to serene yoga sessions on the beach and lively baseball stadium games, there’s no shortage of fun-filled activities – from dancing salsa and cumbia to food tours downtown and much more or events to enjoy as a group. So, gather your friends, embrace the spirit of adventure, and embark on a journey of unforgettable group bonding in the lively and captivating city of Barranquilla.
