The Best Hotels in Barranquilla (2026 Guide)

Mike Chartrand
July 9, 2023 — Updated April 21, 2026 · 3 min read

Barranquilla’s hotel scene has matured considerably in recent years, with international brands joining the city’s historic stalwarts. Whether you’re coming for Carnival, a business trip, or scouting a potential relocation, here are the top hotels worth knowing in 2026 – ranked roughly by quality and positioning.

1. Marriott Hotel Barranquilla

Opened in 2022, the Marriott quickly established itself as Barranquilla’s best full-service luxury option. Located in the Zona Norte near the city’s best restaurants and shopping, it offers exactly what you’d expect from the brand: impeccably appointed rooms, a proper business center, a rooftop pool with city views, and reliable international-standard service. The best hotel in the city for first-time visitors who want zero friction.

  • Best for: Business travelers, first-time visitors, Carnival splurge
  • Location: Zona Norte / Buenavista area
  • Approximate 2026 rate: $400,000–$700,000 COP/night (standard rooms); higher during Carnival

2. Hotel El Prado

Barranquilla’s most iconic hotel and one of the oldest in Colombia, El Prado has been hosting guests since 1930. The colonial architecture – terracotta roofs, arcaded courtyards, a legendary pool – is genuinely beautiful, and the hotel’s location in the historic El Prado neighborhood puts you walking distance from the best of old Barranquilla. It’s a living piece of the city’s history. Service is warm, the breakfast is excellent, and no other hotel in the city has this much character.

  • Best for: Atmosphere seekers, history lovers, those who want to feel the city
  • Location: Historic El Prado neighborhood
  • Approximate 2026 rate: $350,000–$600,000 COP/night

3. Dann Carlton Barranquilla

A five-star Colombian classic that has long been one of the city’s premier business hotels. The Dann Carlton sits directly across from the Buenavista mall complex, making it extremely convenient for shopping and dining. Its standout feature is the rooftop revolving restaurant – one of the few in Colombia – with panoramic city views. Rooms are spacious and well-maintained, service is professional, and the hotel runs smoothly during events like Carnival.

  • Best for: Business trips, groups, location convenience
  • Location: Adjacent to Buenavista mall
  • Approximate 2026 rate: $320,000–$550,000 COP/night

4. Hotel Estelar Alto Prado

Part of the respected Colombian Estelar chain, the Alto Prado is a consistently reliable upscale option located in the Country Club district. It features spacious rooms, a spa, fitness center, and multiple dining options. It attracts a largely Colombian business clientele and consistently delivers a polished experience. A quieter, more residential feel than the Marriott or Dann Carlton.

  • Best for: Extended stays, those who prefer a quieter upscale environment
  • Location: Country Club / Alto Prado district
  • Approximate 2026 rate: $280,000–$480,000 COP/night

5. Hotel Movich Buró 51

A stylish modern boutique-ish hotel in a central location near Viva Mall. Movich positions itself as a design-forward option with a younger, more contemporary feel than the traditional business hotels. The rooftop pool is a highlight, rooms are well-designed and comfortable, and the price-to-quality ratio is solid. A good mid-luxury option if the Marriott and Dann Carlton feel too corporate.

  • Best for: Design-conscious travelers, mid-budget luxury
  • Location: Near Viva Mall, central north
  • Approximate 2026 rate: $250,000–$420,000 COP/night

A Note on Carnival Season

During Carnival week (February 14–17, 2026), all hotel rates in Barranquilla roughly double or triple, and availability disappears months in advance. If you’re planning to come for Carnival, book by November at the latest – ideally earlier. Many hotels require a minimum 3-night stay during Carnival week and may require non-refundable deposits.

Beyond Hotels: Furnished Apartments

For stays of one week or more, furnished apartment rentals often provide significantly more space and a more local experience than hotels – at lower prices. The Zona Norte has dozens of well-managed apartment buildings with pools and gyms where owners list units on Airbnb or through local agencies. For a two-week stay, a comfortable 2BR furnished apartment in Bello Horizonte can cost less than four nights at the Marriott.
