Barranquilla Walking Tour: Stories of El Prado (2026 Review)

Mike Chartrand
April 9, 2026 — Updated April 23, 2026 · 2 min read

El Prado is Barranquilla’s most architecturally remarkable neighbourhood – and one of the most underappreciated historic districts in Colombia. The “Stories of El Prado” walking tour unlocks it: the Art Deco mansions, the Lebanese and Italian immigrant families who built them, the social history of Colombia’s wealthiest city in the first half of the 20th century.

What You’ll See

  • Art Deco and colonial architecture – The mansions of El Prado were built by Barranquilla’s merchant elite in the 1920s–1950s. They’re extraordinary: Moroccan tiles, Italian marble, Lebanese ornament, and a distinctly tropical interpretation of European style.
  • The immigrant story – Barranquilla was Colombia’s main port and received wave after wave of immigrants – Lebanese, Italian, German, Jewish. El Prado’s architecture reflects this directly. Every building has a story.
  • The Hotel El Prado – The neighbourhood’s centrepiece, built in 1930, still operating. The guide explains its history and its role as the social heart of 20th-century Barranquilla.
  • Hidden courtyards, private homes, and streets that most visitors walk past without noticing

Why El Prado Surprises Visitors

Most visitors to Barranquilla stay in El Prado but explore it only superficially – the restaurants and bars. The walking tour reveals the neighbourhood’s depth: it’s as architecturally rich as Cartagena’s old city, but with none of the tourist traffic. You’ll have the streets almost entirely to yourselves.
